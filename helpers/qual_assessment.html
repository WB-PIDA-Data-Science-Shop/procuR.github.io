Qualitative Assessments of Public Procurement Systems

Comparative frameworks and expert evaluations for procurement system performance

This page provides an overview of major qualitative assessment frameworks for public procurement systems. These frameworks offer structured approaches to evaluating regulatory environments, institutional capacity, and operational practices across jurisdictions.

Source Type of Review PP Characteristics Reviewed
WB–OECD MAPS
(example report) 		Legal, regulatory, and policy frameworks Institutional framework and management capacity, procurement operations and market practices, accountability, integrity, and transparency mechanisms
EuroPAM
(scoring system) 		Legal / regulatory Scope, thresholds, transparency, competition, evaluation, institutional setup, and complaint mechanisms
OECD Public Procurement Performance Semi-quantitative reviews of regulations implementation Transparency, publication, sanctions, audit, integrity, payments, efficiency, professionalization, digitalization, GPP, SME
SIGMA Public Procurement Briefs Regulatory + institutional setup Scope and thresholds, planning, procedures, competition and SME access, qualification and exclusion of bidders, award criteria and evaluation, life-cycle costing, modifications, framework agreements and central purchasing, e-procurement, monitoring, audit, PPPs, sustainability
Bosio et al. Comparative expert analysis Regulatory dimensions (transparency, competition, limits to exclusion, integrity of contracts)
